How to Write SEO-Friendly URLs: The Complete Guide

Published on May 10, 2026 • 5 min read
[Ad Space]

A URL is often the first thing Google and your users see. Despite this, many webmasters overlook URL structure, opting for messy, auto-generated strings like ?p=123. If you want to rank higher in search engines, creating clean, SEO-friendly URLs is a fundamental step.

What is a URL Slug?

The "slug" is the part of the URL that identifies a specific page in a human-readable form. For example, in toolsforseo.free.nf/blog/seo-friendly-urls, the slug is seo-friendly-urls.

5 Rules for SEO-Friendly URLs

1. Keep it Short and Simple

Shorter URLs tend to rank better. They are easier to read, copy, paste, and share on social media.

Good: /seo-friendly-urls
Bad: /how-to-write-the-best-seo-friendly-urls-in-2026-guide

2. Include Your Primary Keyword

Your URL should contain the exact keyword you want that page to rank for. Search engines read URLs to understand page context.

3. Use Hyphens, Not Underscores

Google reads hyphens (-) as word separators but not underscores (_). Using underscores means Google reads seo_guide as seoguide.

[Ad Space]

4. Use Lowercase Letters Only

URLs are case-sensitive on some servers. Mixing cases leads to broken links and duplicate content issues. Always use lowercase.

Pro tip: Use our Case Converter Tool to quickly change text to lowercase, or our Text to Slug Tool to auto-generate perfect slugs.

5. Remove Stop Words

Words like "and", "or", "but", "the", "a" add unnecessary length. Search engines ignore them anyway.

Conclusion

Before publishing your next article or product page, take 30 seconds to optimize the slug. It's a tiny effort that yields long-term compounding results in search rankings.
