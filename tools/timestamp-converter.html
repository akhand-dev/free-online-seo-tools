What is a Unix Timestamp Converter?

A Unix timestamp (also known as Epoch time) is the total number of seconds that have elapsed since midnight on January 1, 1970 (UTC). This standardized format is universally used in programming languages, databases, and modern APIs to represent points in time without the complexities of timezones or daylight saving time. Our Unix timestamp converter online simplifies the process of translating these numeric values into human-readable dates, making it an essential debugging tool for developers and engineers.

How to Convert Unix Timestamps

Translating dates to epoch time and vice versa is quick and seamless:

  • Timestamp to Date: Paste your numeric Unix timestamp into the first box and hit convert to see the exact UTC, local time, and ISO 8601 string.
  • Date to Timestamp: Enter a human-readable date format (e.g., "2026-05-19 12:00:00") into the second box to generate the corresponding Unix timestamp and millisecond value.
